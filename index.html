Direct Tax Recent Trends & Amendments

🏢 Concessional Corporate Tax Rates (Sec 115BAA & 115BAB)

- **Section 115BAA**: Existing domestic companies can opt for a **22%** tax rate (effective rate ~25.17% including surcharge & cess) by waiving specific incentives/MAT credit.
- **Section 115BAB**: New manufacturing domestic companies incorporated on or after Oct 1, 2019 can opt for a **15%** tax rate (effective rate ~17.16%).
- **Note**: Once selected, this option cannot be withdrawn in subsequent assessment years.

📈 Minimum Alternate Tax (MAT) Updates (Sec 115JB)

- The basic rate of MAT is **15%** (previously 18.5%) on book profits.
- MAT credit can be carried forward up to **15 Assessment Years** (AY).
- MAT is **not applicable** to companies opting for the concessional tax regime under Section 115BAA or 115BAB.

⚖️ Transfer Pricing & Safe Harbour Rules

- Safe harbour margins are defined for outbound software services, research & development, and low-risk manufacturing.
- Arm's Length Range is applicable if the number of comparables is **6 or more**. The 35th and 65th percentile range is used for calculations.
- Secondary adjustment under Section 92CE is mandatory if primary transfer pricing adjustment exceeds INR 1 Crore.

🌐 Equalisation Levy & Digital Commerce

- **6% Equalisation Levy** applies to consideration paid to non-residents for digital advertising services exceeding INR 1 Lakh.
- **2% Equalisation Levy** applies to e-commerce operators on e-commerce supply/services provided to residents.
- Strict compliance timelines apply: Payments must be deposited monthly by the 7th of the following month.

GST & Cost Audit Regulatory Trends

⚡ GST ITC Matching & GSTR-2B Mandate

- Under CGST Section 16(2)(aa), Input Tax Credit can only be claimed if the supplier has uploaded the details in GSTR-1, and it is reflected in GSTR-2B.
- The previous **5% provisional ITC** buffer has been reduced to **0%**. Strictly match invoices with GSTR-2B to avoid tax penalties.

🧾 E-Invoicing Threshold Extensions

- E-invoicing is mandatory for all taxpayers whose aggregate turnover in any preceding financial year (from 2017-18 onwards) exceeds **INR 5 Crore**.
- Failure to issue IRN (Invoice Reference Number) renders the invoice invalid for ITC claims by the buyer.

📊 ESG Reporting & Cost Records Integration

- Modern cost audit guidelines focus on **Environmental, Social, Governance (ESG)** reporting.
- Cost auditors must review carbon emission credits, water footprints, and resource usage patterns as part of the efficiency audit under Cost Audit Rules.
